Latest news

ALBA initiates its upgrade process

The ALBA Synchrotron has been given the green light to start working on the ALBA II project, an ambitious program that will transform ALBA into a 4th generation synchrotron facility upgrading the accelerator and other components and building new beamlines.

More info

A step forward in the collaboration with portugal

The 17th of December, ALBA organized together with FCT Portugal an online workshop with approximately 100 participants. ALBA presented the Portuguese community of synchrotron users the features and status of the facility. In addition, it also promoted the interaction between Portuguese, Spanish research groups and ALBA scientists to start developing collaborations. In 2021, ALBA will host four postdoctoral positions within this Iberian collaboration which will be based at ALBA premises.

ARIE statement about the founding needs of RIs in the next european program

The Board of the Analytical Research Infrastructures in Europe (ARIE) wish to raise attention to the funding needs of Research Infrastructures (RIs) in the next Horizon Europe (HE) program, and appeal for a reassignment of part of the funds to programs dedicated to Research Infrastructures.

SCIENCE HIGHLIGHTS

The african fly of death migth alse help save lives

For the first time, scientists recreated in the lab the molecule that allows the tsetse fly to feed on blood. It’s a powerful yet small anticoagulant with a unique and strong binding to thrombin, the key enzyme of the coagulation pathway.

Towards more efficient organic solar cells

A new research presents for the first time an experimental methodology based on nanocalorimetry that allows quantifying the degree of intermixing of solar cells, a fundamental knowledge for the design of more efficient and stable devices.

Identified the role of CCT protein in the control of inmune synapse formation

The CCT protein, a cytosolic chaperonin, takes part in the reorganization of the cellular skeleton involved in the immune synapse. This interaction process between lymphocytes and antigen-presenting cells is necessary for the activation of lymphocytes and determines the immune response intensity.

A new material suitable ofr heat-assisted magnetic recording

A new highly-anisotropic Rh-based Heusler compound has been reported as an ideal candidate for high-density magnetic recording media through fast heat-assisted writing technology.

New synthesis procedure to optimize the catalytic performance of platinum based materials

The catalyst showed an initial propane conversion of ~20% and after 70 h the propane conversion decreased very slowly to ~17%, demonstrating the outstanding performance of this new catalyst.

Experimental identification of the nature and magnitude of topological charges in thin films

A group of scientists has been able to identify and measure for the first time the magnitudes of topological charges inside a thin ferromagnetic film, the most commonly used morphology in nanomagnetic devices.

Management board news

Very positive feedback from the Rector Council

On the 16th of December, the Rector Council meeting of ALBA was held. During the meeting, the ALBA II project was presented and approved to start its design. The members of the Rector Council highlighted the great performance of ALBA in 2020, being able to perform the 98% of the scheduled experiments despite the complicated circumstances imposed by covid-19 measurements.

ALBA presented its industrial capabilities to CDTI

The Centre for the Development of Industrial Technology (CDTI) hosted a meeting at the end of November where ALBA summarised its activity with industrial users and available services.
